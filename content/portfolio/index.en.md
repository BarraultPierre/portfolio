### (not) BGM Compilation (2026)

(not) BGM Compilation is my latest released project.
Originally conceived as a portfolio for game music composition, this EP aims to create a cohesive musical universe that mirrors the various moments of a fictional adventure.
To achieve this, I used the *Roland JV-1080*, a synth iconic to the early 2000s gaming aesthetic. I also challenged myself to compose the entire EP in just two weeks, pushing my creativity and experimenting with the pressures of a tight deadline.

### Eyes Without a Face (2024)
This project is an original score for a scene from Georges Franju’s *Eyes Without a Face* that was originally silent. 
To amplify the film’s unsettling atmosphere, I drew inspiration from Steve Reich’s polyrhythms, creating a disorienting yet hypnotic soundscape, as well as Bernard Herrmann’s signature use of the major seventh interval, a powerful tool for building relentless tension. I also worked within specific artistic and technical constraints: weaving the film’s existing leitmotif into my composition, limiting instrumentation to a small ensemble and the Hammond organ to stay true to the 1960s sound, and carefully crafting the sound design to artificially age the recordings—adding analog warmth and saturation to match the era’s sonic texture.

### Lise Terre and Lise Nuit (2023)
This project is an original soundtrack composed for an initiatory tale intended for children.
Music for children is generally composed using organic instruments; it is an opportunity for composers to present the different families of instruments such as brass, string instruments, and woodwinds. Here, my intention was to introduce children to electronic music by using synthesizers with a maximum of two oscillators and simple waveforms: limiting myself to sine, square, and triangle waves, which are the fundamental waveforms of electronic music.

To compose, I drew inspiration from three artists:

First, Claude Larson, who demonstrated that synthesizers can also be vectors of emotion—a notion I hold dear. In the context of music for children, I don’t think it’s very interesting to create irony with melody; the only function this soundtrack needed to fulfill was to accompany the action and highlight the emotion. The philosophy of this music was therefore for children to be able to associate electronic music with feelings.

For the pure composition, I was inspired by Prokofiev. I associated themes and synthesizers with the characters; these themes evolve depending on the action. For example, the themes of Little Brother and Little Sister are linked when they are together and separate when they find themselves apart. Miel, who represents the mentor in Little Brother’s initiation, has a theme close to his, etc.

Finally, I drew inspiration from Steve Reich, who is a pillar in electronic music culture. I mention him several times by using polyrhythms. This technique allows me to maintain tension and connect different scenes.

### Insadium (2022)
Insadium is a series of 7 introspective portraits. I composed a soundtrack that accompanies the enigmatic and painful narration of these works.
The music evolves with the portraits, alternating between moments of calm and tension, to highlight their emotional contrasts.
The goal is to create total immersion, where the viewer is immersed into an experience that is both visual and auditory.
