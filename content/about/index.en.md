## About me

Introduced to piano and music theory from a very young age, I discovered my passion for music there.

Very quickly, I explored computer-based music production. I already enjoyed composing while learning piano, but the vast creative possibilities offered by DAWs won me over.

Initially, I was headed toward computer engineering studies (I’ve always loved tinkering with computers) but during my studies, I questioned this path. I felt that, on a human level, I needed to contribute meaningfully, to find a field where I could give the best of myself.
I then turned to a degree in sound engineering, a program that would allow me to develop a comprehensive approach as a composer.

My learning of music theory has been mostly empirical and self-taught over the years. I first discovered composition techniques, and then, through research, I was able to identify them: cadences, scale degrees, harmonic progressions, etc.
In DAW-based production, since composition alone isn’t enough, I did extensive research on mixing, mastering, and sound design, knowledge that my studies later complemented.

Today, I aim to showcase my skills and my creativity.

## Inspiration
